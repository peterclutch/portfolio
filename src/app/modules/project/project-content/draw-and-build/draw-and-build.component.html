In collaboration with LEGO, we were given the objective of brainstorming new ideas and developing proof-of-concepts for rethinking ways for children to play and create using LEGO bricks.

In this context, I chose to experiment with projecting media onto LEGO bricks to investigate which qualities it might encompass.

Projection Mapping

Working with projection mapping is by no means an easy feat.

  1. The environment needs to be ideal in terms of lighting and placement of equipment—you also need to consider how to deal with occlusion
  2. The intrinsic and extrinsic parameters of the camera and projector needs to be calibrated carefully (recalibration has to be done every time the set up changes the slightest)
  3. You are severely limited by your hardware—which is rather costly in the first place

In the end, we successfully created a functional prototype which could display children's drawings onto a brick within a limited field of view (restricted by the camera and/or projector).

The color-code at the top of a tracker-brick is used for identification and tracking in three-dimensional space. We then know which drawing should be projects onto the brick, the size of the projection and its location.

Afterwards, we still had to implement an application to dynamically save and select media (i.e. children's drawings) to be projected. Given the tight deadlines of a university project and the difficulties of bringing projection mapping to exhibitions and after-school centres we had to pivot the concept.

Draw and Build

The final—less ambitious—concept had a reduced scope while still trying to solve the same research question:

What are the benefits of integrating drawing and brick building into a single creative activity?

To solve this research question, Draw and Build was created. Using a camera and projector embedded into a LEGO box, children's paintings could be projected directly onto a 3D construction canvas in real time.

Our research highlighted that integrating drawing with brick building enhances the personalization of children’s creations.
